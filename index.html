Research Question

How does the spread of misinformation vary from subreddit to subreddit, and what is the relationship between these subreddits?

  • What can user activity tell us about trends within subreddits and users who commonly post misinformation?

Background & Prior Work

Social media has grown in popularity and has massive implications for how people are exposed to information and the quality of the information they interact with. Reddit is a pre-categorized form of social media, through the use of subreddits, and provides a way to analyze the quality of information given a certain category or topic.

Our previous project analyzed misinformation networks on twitter using tweets related to COVID-19. However, this is just one topic, and maybe the frequency of misinformation varies from topic to topic. For example, a subreddit thatâ€™s used for discussing Linux software is probably less likely to be spreading misinformation compared to a political subreddit.

Hypothesis

Subreddits leaning more towards sensitive topics, such as politics, news etc. are more likely to be spreading misinformation. In addition, these subreddits may share a lot of the same users.

Data Wrangling

We are trying to systematically create a way to identify sources of misinformation. Currently, if a user posts a misinfo url, the script will get the subreddits they comment the most on. We then compared those subreddits to the other users who post misinfo too. After that, we can see which subreddits they have the most in common.
